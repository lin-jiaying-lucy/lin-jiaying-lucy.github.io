Design of an electronic organ

An general Introduction of my electronic organ

There are 8 buttons that raise the pitch from left to right, barely discerning an octave

Reasons for making an electronic organ

I am good at playing the piano, and the principle of piano sound production is pure mechanical motion, which makes the strings vibrate and sound through tapping. The length, diameter, and thickness of the strings can both affect the frequency of the sound, thereby affecting the pitch. Then, I want to try combining the piano with electronics, which means to make an electronic piano

Use of principles of physics and my train of thought

I learned online that LC circuits generate sine waves through oscillation, and electromagnetic buzzers can convert sine waves into sound. I also learned about the NE555 chip online and built this typical oscillation circuit with the 555 chip as the core.

I also learned a formula for RC circuits: t=ln2(R1+R2)C, so the easiest way is to change resistance value, thereby changing the cycle and tone. So when the switch is pressed, the resistance corresponding to the button is shorted, the resistance decreases, the cycle shortens, and the frequency increases

Stage of soldering tin

Solder components from low to high

Then solder the components to the corresponding positions on the PCB board without any false soldering, which is very good

Areas that can be improved

The pitch of the electronic organ is very inaccurate, although the increase in pitch can be clearly heard, it cannot play a complete piece of music.

I think the first reason is the resistance value is not accurate enough. If I want to improve it, I can add a sliding resistor or combine ir with Arduino because the crystal oscillator of Arduino can generate a reference frequency to ensure the accuracy of the frequency .

Secondly, The electromagnetic buzzer doesn’t sound good, I can use horn to replace the buzzer. The horn amplifies the signal and uses the diaphragm to make the air vibrate and sound, while the buzzer controls the voltage to make the magnet vibrate and sound, resulting in a richer sound quality of the horn.

Thirdly, After completing the production, I learned that a real electronic organ would record the original sound of the instrument and play it by playing back the original sound through buttons. This is also a very clever method.
