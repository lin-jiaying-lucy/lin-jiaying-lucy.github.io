air-conditioning sleeping bag

Air conditioning sleeping bag creativity

General introduction

Using semiconductor refrigeration components to replace traditional compressors and refrigerants, achieving the goal of small size and low electricity consumption. So as to make two refrigeration devices. Install these two refrigeration devices in a small box to create air conditioning, and send the air conditioning into the sleeping bag through a pipe. The final air outlet is 4 degrees lower than the ambient temperature, and the plan is feasible

Inspiration

During an extracurricular practice activity organized by a learning organization, I slept in a sleeping bag. Although there was air conditioning in the dormitory, I still felt very stuffy and my feet were very hot , So I want to design such an air-conditioned sleeping bag. In addition, sleeping bag air conditioners are more energy-efficient, environmentally friendly, small in size, and easy to carry than air conditioners. This design is very practical for camping tents , Because the power consumption is low, you can use a mobile power supply

Some details of production process

1.When discussing the connection between the refrigeration plate and the thermal and cooling blocks, applying silicone can increase the contact area, thereby improving the conduction efficiency

  1. I think the most difficult part is not assembling the refrigeration module, but designing the box. If the box is well designed, it can better reduce the temperature. According to Bernoulli’s law, connecting pipes on both sides of a box with a small cross-sectional area can transport gas faster. In addition, the selection of pipes and fans is also worth considering.

  2. For this design, if it is winter, perhaps the refrigeration device can be placed over and installed, with the heat dissipation end facing towards the inside of the box to create heating

electronic organ

Design of an electronic organ

An general Introduction of my electronic organ

There are 8 buttons that raise the pitch from left to right, barely discerning an octave

Reasons for making an electronic organ

I am good at playing the piano, and the principle of piano sound production is pure mechanical motion, which makes the strings vibrate and sound through tapping. The length, diameter, and thickness of the strings can both affect the frequency of the sound, thereby affecting the pitch. Then, I want to try combining the piano with electronics, which means to make an electronic piano

Use of principles of physics and my train of thought

I learned online that LC circuits generate sine waves through oscillation, and electromagnetic buzzers can convert sine waves into sound. I also learned about the NE555 chip online and built this typical oscillation circuit with the 555 chip as the core.

I also learned a formula for RC circuits: t=ln2(R1+R2)C, so the easiest way is to change resistance value, thereby changing the cycle and tone. So when the switch is pressed, the resistance corresponding to the button is shorted, the resistance decreases, the cycle shortens, and the frequency increases

Stage of soldering tin

Solder components from low to high

Then solder the components to the corresponding positions on the PCB board without any false soldering, which is very good

[![pPgBk59.jpg](https://s1.ax1x.com/2023/09/11/pPgBk59.jpg

Areas that can be improved

The pitch of the electronic organ is very inaccurate, although the increase in pitch can be clearly heard, it cannot play a complete piece of music.

I think the first reason is the resistance value is not accurate enough. If I want to improve it, I can add a sliding resistor or combine ir with Arduino because the crystal oscillator of Arduino can generate a reference frequency to ensure the accuracy of the frequency .

Secondly, The electromagnetic buzzer doesn’t sound good, I can use horn to replace the buzzer. The horn amplifies the signal and uses the diaphragm to make the air vibrate and sound, while the buzzer controls the voltage to make the magnet vibrate and sound, resulting in a richer sound quality of the horn.

Thirdly, After completing the production, I learned that a real electronic organ would record the original sound of the instrument and play it by playing back the original sound through buttons. This is also a very clever method.

automatic watering system

general Introduction of my design

There are many plants at home. One day, I saw a DIY automatic watering system online, and I was very interested in it. I purchased some electronic components online for wiring.

When the detector detects low soil moisture, the water pump starts working and delivers the water from the basin to the plants through a pipe

Main principles

Due to different soil moisture levels, the resistance values measured by the detector may vary. At a certain resistance value, the sensor will give a small current to the electromagnetic relay (there is an arduino board between the sensor and the electromagnetic relay), and the electromagnetic relay will control the water pump to water the flowers. At the same time, the arduino can display the humidity and water pump status on the LCD

first version(without arduino)

My first version mainly focused on the circuit design of the watering system and did not include an arduino board

final version(with arduino)

Then I added the arduino board

With the addition of Arduino boards, I have also made some improvements to the circuit. In the first version, there are two power supplies, Power supply A supplies power to the arduino and sensors for control purposes. Power supply B is connected to the common and normally closed ends of the electromagnetic relay, as well as the water pump, to provide power. After turning on power supply B, the normally closed end is closed and the water pump starts working. At this point, if the soil is sufficiently wet, the sensor will send an electrical signal to disconnect the normally closed end and stop the water pump from working.

However, I discovered 2 problems. firstly, If power supply A is unexpectedly cut off and the circuit loses control, the water pump will continue to operate under power supply B. secondly, Through common sense in daily life, we can know that the time when the watering pump does not work is much longer than the working time. When the water pump stops working, the normally closed end needs to be disconnected, which means that it requires electricity consumption to attract the magnet and overcome the spring force in the relay. This is wasteful and will shorten the lifespan of the relay.

I have made some modifications to the circuit. Modification1. Remove power supply B and directly connect power to the electromagnetic relay and water pump from the arduino board (power supply A is connected to the arduino board), which means that both control and power electricity come from the same power source. This solves problem 1. Modification 2: Modify the program by swapping the logic of the electrical signal section and then continuing with the normal start, so that the relay does not need to consume power when the water pump is not working. Problem 2 is resolved

Design of a parking sensor

A general introduction of my design

I designed a simple Parking sensor. The entire process of document production involves first searching for open source code online, then purchasing components, wiring the code, and finally understanding the code and making some minor modifications.

When the distance between the radar and the obstacle is less than 50cm, the buzzer will sound and the red LED light on the nano will flash. LCD1602 screen will display distance and Progress bar. When the distance is less than 50cm, the screen will display ‘DANGER’, and when the distance is more than 100cm, it will display ‘OK’

Unfortunately, the photos were not taken completely and no obstacles were found

Reasons for doing this project

Recently, I have been very interested in the Arduino platform. I heard that it is an easy to learn and open source platform. This is very friendly and suitable for beginners like me, and I can implement some creative projects through Arduino. I bought a book to learn Arduino, and then I plan to practice some knowledge by making a Parking sensor with Arduino.

A specific introduction of each part

Hardware

Ultrasonic ranging:

In junior high school physics, ultrasound can be used for ranging. In this project, I found open source code online. Then I learned through reading the code that the TRIG pin of the ultrasound module is connected to the 5th pin of the nano, and the ECHO is connected to the 6th pin.

LCD

I learned from the book that the SDA on the LDC is a data cable connected to the A4 of the nano. SCL is the clock line, connected to the A5 of the nano.

When I connected the cable and passed the program into Arduino, I found that the LCD screen was on, but there was no data display at all. I went from coding to wiring inspection. Finally, I found that because of the contrast problem, after adjusting the Potentiometer with a screwdriver, the words on the screen were displayed successfully. This is too funny!

Buzzer

This is a simple little thing, just connect its positive pole to 12. But I was too careless. I confused the 12 on the Breadboard with the 12 on the nano. Fortunately, I soon realized this problem

software

The display screen is the first line in common sense, and the 0th line in programming. This point needs to be noted

space—vehicle

**Design of a space vehicle

A general introduction of my design

This car is used to participate in a creative technology competition called solving for tomorrow, organized by the China Association for Science and Technology. The rules of the competition are to determine who’s car can climb over obstacles at a higher height with limited weight and size. At the competition site, contestants can only bring in small car parts, and the motors and batteries will be distributed uniformly in the competition. Contestants need to assemble the small car on site.

It is worth mentioning that almost all materials are recycled

In the end, I won the second prize at the provincial level.

Each part of my design

Counterweight (small medicine bottle)

There are iron nails in the small medicine bottle because when the leg of the car touches an obstacle, the car cannot drive the entire car up due to insufficient motor power. At this time, the medicine bottle can provide a helping force. Metals have high density, weight, and small volume. The number of iron nails is the optimal value obtained through experiments, which will not burden the car too much and can also help the car climb over

The legs of the car

The leg design is what I feel most proud of. At the beginning, my thinking was always to make the legs longer and better, so that the car could climb higher. At first, it worked, but later I found that as the legs grew longer, the strength of the legs decreased. After reaching the obstacle, the legs had no strength to climb up because the power of the motor was limited

By recording videos and watching slow playback, I designed three legs each with their own functions. When the car is in this state as shown in the picture, the lengthened legs support the ground, and the climbing legs have leather covers and glue at the front end, thereby increasing the friction force. Then the power legs came on stage, and due to the support of the glasses legs and the effect of the clipping legs, the power legs could reach obstacles even if they were very short. The power legs are very short, but they are very powerful and can lift the entire car onto obstacles

Car support point (glasses)

The support of the car used to be two wooden rods, but due to the limited size of the car, the length of the wooden rods was also limited. However, if the support rods are not long enough, the car will fall backwards when climbing over a high obstacle. And the glasses legs can be folded, and when climbing a slope, the glasses legs open to support the ground. When measuring the size of the car, the glasses legs are folded

Inside the car

There is an electric motor inside the car. Due to the poor quality of the motor, during the bumping process of the car, the motor slipped. Finally, a screw was inserted next to the motor to tighten the gear. I am fortunate to have discovered this issue during experiments, rather than during competitions. This gave me inspiration that before completing a project, it is necessary to conduct multiple experiments to expose the problem in the experimental stage
