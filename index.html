parking sensor

Design of a parking sensor

A general introduction of my design

I designed a simple Parking sensor. The entire process of document production involves first searching for open source code online, then purchasing components, wiring the code, and finally understanding the code and making some minor modifications.

When the distance between the radar and the obstacle is less than 50cm, the buzzer will sound and the red LED light on the nano will flash. LCD1602 screen will display distance and Progress bar. When the distance is less than 50cm, the screen will display ‘DANGER’, and when the distance is more than 100cm, it will display ‘OK’

Unfortunately, the photos were not taken completely and no obstacles were found

Reasons for doing this project

Recently, I have been very interested in the Arduino platform. I heard that it is an easy to learn and open source platform. This is very friendly and suitable for beginners like me, and I can implement some creative projects through Arduino. I bought a book to learn Arduino, and then I plan to practice some knowledge by making a Parking sensor with Arduino.

A specific introduction of each part

Hardware

Ultrasonic ranging:

In junior high school physics, ultrasound can be used for ranging. In this project, I found open source code online. Then I learned through reading the code that the TRIG pin of the ultrasound module is connected to the 5th pin of the nano, and the ECHO is connected to the 6th pin.

LCD

I learned from the book that the SDA on the LDC is a data cable connected to the A4 of the nano. SCL is the clock line, connected to the A5 of the nano.

When I connected the cable and passed the program into Arduino, I found that the LCD screen was on, but there was no data display at all. I went from coding to wiring inspection. Finally, I found that because of the contrast problem, after adjusting the Potentiometer with a screwdriver, the words on the screen were displayed successfully. This is too funny!

Buzzer

This is a simple little thing, just connect its positive pole to 12. But I was too careless. I confused the 12 on the Breadboard with the 12 on the nano. Fortunately, I soon realized this problem

software

The above two images are source code, and I have made some Chinese annotations on them after understanding them. The following image is a modification I made to the display of the LCD screen.

The display screen is the first line in common sense, and the 0th line in programming. This point needs to be noted

**Design of a space vehicle

A general introduction of my design

This car is used to participate in a creative technology competition called solving for tomorrow, organized by the China Association for Science and Technology. The rules of the competition are to determine who’s car can climb over obstacles at a higher height with limited weight and size. At the competition site, contestants can only bring in small car parts, and the motors and batteries will be distributed uniformly in the competition. Contestants need to assemble the small car on site.

It is worth mentioning that almost all materials are recycled

In the end, I won the second prize at the provincial level.

Each part of my design

Counterweight (small medicine bottle)

There are iron nails in the small medicine bottle because when the leg of the car touches an obstacle, the car cannot drive the entire car up due to insufficient motor power. At this time, the medicine bottle can provide a helping force. Metals have high density, weight, and small volume. The number of iron nails is the optimal value obtained through experiments, which will not burden the car too much and can also help the car climb over

The legs of the car

The leg design is what I feel most proud of. At the beginning, my thinking was always to make the legs longer and better, so that the car could climb higher. At first, it worked, but later I found that as the legs grew longer, the strength of the legs decreased. After reaching the obstacle, the legs had no strength to climb up because the power of the motor was limited

By recording videos and watching slow playback, I designed three legs each with their own functions. When the car is in this state as shown in the picture, the lengthened legs support the ground, and the climbing legs have leather covers and glue at the front end, thereby increasing the friction force. Then the power legs came on stage, and due to the support of the glasses legs and the effect of the clipping legs, the power legs could reach obstacles even if they were very short. The power legs are very short, but they are very powerful and can lift the entire car onto obstacles

Car support point (glasses)

The support of the car used to be two wooden rods, but due to the limited size of the car, the length of the wooden rods was also limited. However, if the support rods are not long enough, the car will fall backwards when climbing over a high obstacle. And the glasses legs can be folded, and when climbing a slope, the glasses legs open to support the ground. When measuring the size of the car, the glasses legs are folded

Inside the car

There is an electric motor inside the car. Due to the poor quality of the motor, during the bumping process of the car, the motor slipped. Finally, a screw was inserted next to the motor to tighten the gear. I am fortunate to have discovered this issue during experiments, rather than during competitions. This gave me inspiration that before completing a project, it is necessary to conduct multiple experiments to expose the problem in the experimental stage

4.Markdown语法介绍

在前面的课程中，我们已经成功搭建了我们的blog，并将它放到了github通过域名进行访问，今天我们来学习一下如何用Markdown来写文章。

首先简要介绍一下什么是Markdown：

  • Markdown 是一种轻量级标记语言，它允许人们使用易读易写的纯文本格式编写文档。

  • Markdown 语言在 2004 由约翰·格鲁伯（英语：John Gruber）创建。

  • Markdown 编写的文档可以导出 HTML 、Word、图像、PDF、Epub 等多种格式的文档。

  • Markdown 编写的文档后缀为 .md, .markdown。
